The UnplugJourney approach

Most families do not need a harsher set of screen rules. They need a repeatable way to make technology choices that match their values, seasons, ages, and real-life energy levels.

01

Notice the pattern

Where is tech helping, distracting, soothing, or replacing connection?

02

Choose one boundary

Start with a small rule your family can keep for the next seven days.

03

Replace, don’t just remove

Pair screen limits with rituals, activities, or tools that make offline time easier.

04

Review without shame

Adjust the plan based on what worked, what didn’t, and what your family needs next.

Glossary

Plain-language digital wellness terms

Digital detox

An intentional break from screens, apps, or digital noise so your family can reset habits and reconnect offline.

Intentional AI

Using AI tools with a clear purpose, family boundaries, privacy awareness, and human judgment.

Family media plan

A shared agreement for when, where, why, and how technology fits into family life.

Tech-free zone

A room, table, routine, or time block where devices are put away to protect connection, rest, or focus.

FAQ

Questions this new site should answer first

Is UnplugJourney anti-technology?

No. The point is balance. The site helps families reduce digital overwhelm while still using helpful tools, including AI, with intention.

Where should a busy parent start?

Start with one small reset: a tech-free meal, a screen-free bedtime step, or a family conversation about what technology is helping and what it is interrupting.

Will this include AI parenting resources?

Yes. Intentional AI for Families is a major pillar, including safety checklists, age-appropriate boundaries, conversation starters, and family AI guidelines.
